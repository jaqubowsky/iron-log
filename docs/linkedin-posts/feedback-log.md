# LinkedIn Post Feedback Log

Patterns learned from Jakub's edits and feedback during post writing.

- 2026-03-22: Don't spend first 2/3 of post referencing someone else's content. Jakub's audience wants to know what HE thinks/does. Video/source reference should be 1-2 sentences max, then move to his own setup.
- 2026-03-22: Include a personal result/outcome, not just process description. "I built two full NestJS modules from scratch" > "I understand concepts I used to skim over". Specific > vague.
- 2026-03-22: Don't frame it as "stopping AI" — he's changing how he uses it, not quitting. "Not quitting AI. The opposite - once I actually understand what I'm looking at, I'll get way more out of it."
- 2026-03-22: Don't expose specific knowledge gaps that look bad to recruiters (e.g. "I didn't know CREATE TABLE syntax after 4 years"). Frame as growth instead.
- 2026-03-22: Hook from effect, not from source. "I've been using AI to learn code for months. Turns out I wasn't learning much." > "I watched a video about..."
- 2026-03-22: One moment of emotion/honesty is enough. "It's annoying. You're stuck on something, the AI has the answer right there, and it won't give it to you." — don't overdo it, one is plenty.
- 2026-03-22: Results paragraph should be specific and show scale, not just one concept. "I built two full NestJS modules from scratch - database, relations, error handling" > "I finally get how DI works"
- 2026-03-22: Don't add a second sentence that weakens a strong closer. "And I can explain every line, not just make it compile" is strong — adding "I'm also starting to question my own code" dilutes it.
- 2026-03-22: Avoid "copy-paste" framing that implies he was incompetent before. "Struggled way more than I expected" > "just sat there" (total helplessness).
- 2026-03-22: Post length for complex topics: ~10-12 sentences works. His simple posts are 4-8, but building-in-public posts need more room.
- 2026-03-22: When referencing a video/article, tag the author naturally in the sentence ("I watched a video by @Name about...") instead of a separate "shoutout to @Name" line. Author gets a notification, feels natural, not performative.
- 2026-03-22: In saved posts, mark LinkedIn tags as @First Last — so when copying to LinkedIn you remember who to tag.
